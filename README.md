## Business Context

Residents in North DFW — Frisco, McKinney, Allen, Prosper, Celina, and Plano — are facing a housing affordability crisis. Home prices have more than doubled over the past decade while wage growth has struggled to keep pace.

This project was commissioned as an independent analysis to answer five key questions:

1. Are we heading toward a housing market correction in North DFW?
2. Who is most financially at risk?
3. Is the wage gap a primary driver of the crisis?
4. What does the next 2-3 years look like if nothing changes?
5. What interventions could realistically help?

## Notebooks

### ✅ Notebook 01 — Data Ingestion
Loads and stages all six datasets. Filters to North DFW zip codes. Reshapes Zillow data from wide to long format. Resamples mortgage rates from weekly to monthly.

### 🔜 Notebook 02 — Data Cleaning & Wrangling
Merges all datasets into a master dataframe. Handles missing values and temporal alignment across annual, monthly, and 5-year datasets.

### 🔜 Notebook 03 — Feature Engineering
Builds affordability ratios, price-to-income ratios, mortgage burden scores, and population pressure indexes by zip code.

### 🔜 Notebook 04 — Predictive Model
Trains a Random Forest / XGBoost model to forecast home prices and affordability index 2-3 years out. Generates risk scores by zip code.

### 🔜 Notebook 05 — Model Evaluation & Tableau Export
Evaluates model performance. Exports clean data for Tableau dashboard visualization.
